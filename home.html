Coldplay are a Briitsh rock band, formed in London in 1996, and consist of five members; Chris Martin, Jonny Buckland, Guy Berryman, Will Champion and Phil Harvey. The band had originally started with Martin and Buckland during orientation week during their university days during the autumn days of September 1996. The duo started off as Pectoralz. A year later, Guy Berryman, a classmate of theirs, joined with Martin and Buckland. By 1997, the group renamed as Starfish, and performed gigs aroudn London. Chris Martin recruited a longtime school friend, Phil Harvey, as the fourth member, and Will Champion as the fifth member. After forming their line-up, the band finally set on the name Coldplay.

The musical style of Coldplay generally fall under a few categories; alternative rock, pop rock, post-Britpop, pop, alternate pop and soft rock. Over two decades, the band has also experimented with other genres, including electropop, synth-pop, indie rock, psychedlic, indie electronic, EDM and R&B. Coldplay's music has also been referred to as mediative, limestone rock, and blue romantic. Their style have been influenced from among artists like Echo & the Bunnymen, Kate Bush, and Beatles member George Harrison, and other elements like grafitti and the German resistance group White Rose.

In light of how the band have become, Coldplay rarely allowed product endorsements, and turned down multimillion dollar contracts from brands that wished to use their songs. In terms of philanthropy, Coldplay were known to give 10% of the band's profit to charity in their early days, as well as supporting charity events for cancer, climate change, and disaster relief events. In addition, the band have made some political endorsements for US presidential candidates in 2004 and 2008, and for the UK to remain in the European Union in the 2016 Brexit referendum.
